 History of LOUVRE MUSEUM: 

 

The Louvre Museum, located in the heart of Paris along the River Seine, is one of the most iconic cultural landmarks in the world.
Originally built in the late 12th century as a defensive fortress, the structure was later transformed into a royal palace and 
eventually became a public museum in 1793 after the French Revolution. Over the centuries, the Louvre has grown not only in size but
also in importance, evolving into a symbol of France‚Äôs artistic, historical, and cultural heritage. Today, it houses thousands of artworks 
that span from ancient civilizations to the 19th century, making it a living record of human creativity and history.
What makes the Louvre truly unique is the extraordinary range and scale of its collections combined with its architecture. Inside the museum, 
visitors can see world-famous masterpieces such as the Mona Lisa, the Venus de Milo, and the Winged Victory of Samothrace, alongside Egyptian 
antiquities, Islamic art, Renaissance paintings, sculptures, and decorative arts. The museum itself is a blend of old and new, with the historic
palace walls contrasting beautifully with the modern glass pyramid at its entrance. This pyramid has become a global symbol of Paris, representing 
the harmony between tradition and innovation.
